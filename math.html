Probability 4/5 Marks
Q5: Bag I contains 4 red and 4 black balls. Bag II contains 2 red and 6 black balls. One of the two bags is selected at random and a ball is drawn from it, which is found to be red. Find the probability that the ball is drawn from Bag I.

Why it is important: Bayes' Theorem ka ye 'Bags and Balls' wala question CBSE ka all-time favorite hai.

Formula Used: \( P(E_1|A) = \frac{P(E_1)P(A|E_1)}{P(E_1)P(A|E_1) + P(E_2)P(A|E_2)} \)

Step 1: Define events.
\( E_1 \): Bag I is chosen. \( P(E_1) = 1/2 \)
\( E_2 \): Bag II is chosen. \( P(E_2) = 1/2 \)
A: Drawing a red ball.

Step 2: Find conditional probabilities.
\( P(A|E_1) \) = Probability of red from Bag I = \( 4/8 = 1/2 \)
\( P(A|E_2) \) = Probability of red from Bag II = \( 2/8 = 1/4 \)

Step 3: Put values in Bayes' Formula to find \( P(E_1|A) \).

Calculation: \( \frac{(1/2 \times 1/2)}{(1/2 \times 1/2) + (1/2 \times 1/4)} = \frac{1/4}{1/4 + 1/8} = \frac{1/4}{3/8} \)

Final Answer: \( 2/3 \)

Differential Equations 4 Marks
Q6: Solve the linear differential equation:

\( x \frac{dy}{dx} + 2y = x^2 \)

Why it is important: Linear Differential Equation (LDE) ka format har board paper mein dikhta hai.

Concept: Equation ko standard form \( \frac{dy}{dx} + Py = Q \) mein lao. Phir Integrating Factor (IF) nikalo: \( IF = e^{\int P dx} \).

Step 1: Divide by \( x \) to make coefficient of dy/dx unity.
\( \frac{dy}{dx} + (\frac{2}{x})y = x \)

Step 2: Here \( P = \frac{2}{x} \) and \( Q = x \).

Step 3: Find IF.
\( IF = e^{\int \frac{2}{x} dx} = e^{2 \log x} = e^{\log x^2} = x^2 \)

Step 4: Solution is given by \( y \times IF = \int (Q \times IF) dx + C \).
\( y \times x^2 = \int (x \times x^2) dx + C \)
\( y x^2 = \int x^3 dx + C \)

Final Answer: \( y x^2 = \frac{x^4}{4} + C \)

Relations & Functions 3 Marks
Q7: Show that the relation R in the set Z of integers given by \( R = \{(a, b) : 2 \text{ divides } (a - b)\} \) is an equivalence relation.

Why it is important: Equivalence relation prove karne ka ye sabse standard aur repeated question hai.

Concept: Ek relation equivalence tab hota hai jab wo Reflexive (a,a), Symmetric (a,b implies b,a), aur Transitive (a,b & b,c implies a,c) ho.

Reflexive: For any \( a \in Z \), \( a - a = 0 \), which is divisible by 2. So, \( (a, a) \in R \).

Symmetric: Let \( (a, b) \in R \). Then 2 divides \( (a - b) \). This means \( (a - b) = 2k \). So, \( (b - a) = -2k \), which is also divisible by 2. Hence, \( (b, a) \in R \).

Transitive: Let \( (a, b) \in R \) and \( (b, c) \in R \). Then \( a - b = 2m \) and \( b - c = 2n \). Adding them: \( (a - b) + (b - c) = 2m + 2n \Rightarrow a - c = 2(m + n) \). This is divisible by 2. So, \( (a, c) \in R \).

Final Answer: Since R is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive, it is an equivalence relation.

Application of Derivatives 5 Marks
Q8: Show that of all rectangles inscribed in a given fixed circle, the square has the maximum area.

Why it is important: Maxima-Minima ke word problems 5-marks section mein sure-shot aate hain.

Concept: Area function banao 1 variable mein. First derivative nikalo aur 0 ke equal put karke critical point nikalo. Phir Second Derivative Test se maximum prove karo (\( A'' < 0 \)).

Step 1: Let circle radius be \( a \) (constant). Let rectangle sides be \( x \) and \( y \). By Pythagoras in the circle, \( x^2 + y^2 = (2a)^2 = 4a^2 \). So, \( y = \sqrt{4a^2 - x^2} \).

Step 2: Area \( A = x \cdot y = x\sqrt{4a^2 - x^2} \). [Trick: A ko differentiate karna mushkil hai, toh \( Z = A^2 \) ko maximize karo, same answer aayega!]

Step 3: \( Z = x^2(4a^2 - x^2) = 4a^2x^2 - x^4 \).

Step 4: Differentiate: \( \frac{dZ}{dx} = 8a^2x - 4x^3 \). Put \( \frac{dZ}{dx} = 0 \) to get \( 4x(2a^2 - x^2) = 0 \). Since x can't be 0, \( x^2 = 2a^2 \), so \( x = \sqrt{2}a \).

Step 5: Check second derivative: \( \frac{d^2Z}{dx^2} = 8a^2 - 12x^2 \). Put \( x^2 = 2a^2 \), we get \( 8a^2 - 24a^2 = -16a^2 < 0 \). Hence, it is a Maximum.

Step 6: Put \( x^2 = 2a^2 \) in \( x^2 + y^2 = 4a^2 \). We get \( y^2 = 2a^2 \), so \( y = \sqrt{2}a \). Since \( x = y \), the rectangle is a square!

Step 1: Find f(2).
f(2) = k(2)2 = 4k

Step 2: Find RHL (x > 2).
lim (x→2+) f(x) = 3

Concept: Parabola draw karo, focus (a,0) mark karo. Latus rectum x=a ki line hoti hai. Symmetry ka use karke 1st quadrant ka area nikalo aur 2 se multiply kar do.



Step 1: Parabola y2 = 4ax is symmetric about x-axis. Latus rectum is the line x = a.

Step 2: Total Area = 2 × (Area in 1st quadrant from x=0 to x=a).

Step 3: Extract y in terms of x: y = √(4ax) = 2√a √x.

Probability Distribution 4 Marks
Q13: A random variable X has the following probability distribution:

X : 0 | 1 | 2 | 3
P(X) : 0.1 | k | 2k | 2k

Find the value of k and the Mean of X.

Why it is important: Probability Distribution table se 'k' ki value aur Mean nikalne ka seedha sawal hamesha aata hai.

Concept: Sum of all probabilities hamesha 1 hota hai (ΣP(x) = 1). Aur Mean ka formula hai μ = Σ[x × P(x)].

Step 1: Use ΣP(x) = 1.
0.1 + k + 2k + 2k = 1
0.1 + 5k = 1
5k = 0.9
k = 0.18

Step 2: Substitute k back in the table.
P(X=0) = 0.1
P(X=1) = 0.18
P(X=2) = 0.36
P(X=3) = 0.36

Step 3: Calculate Mean (ΣxP(x)).
Mean = (0 × 0.1) + (1 × 0.18) + (2 × 0.36) + (3 × 0.36)
Mean = 0 + 0.18 + 0.72 + 1.08 = 1.98

Final Answer: k = 0.18, Mean = 1.98

3D Geometry 5 Marks
Q14: Find the coordinates of the foot of the perpendicular drawn from the point P(1, 2, 3) to the line:

(x - 6)/3 = (y - 7)/2 = (z - 7)/-2

Why it is important: Foot of perpendicular aur Image nikalne wala question 3D Geometry ka sabse lamba aur sabse important 5-mark question hai.

Concept: Line ki equation ko 'λ' ke equal maano. Wahan se general point (foot) nikalo. Phir given point aur foot ke beech ke direction ratios (DRs) nikalo. Kyunki line aur perpendicular 90 degree par hain, unke DRs ka dot product 0 hoga (a1a2 + b1b2 + c1c2 = 0).



Step 1: Let (x - 6)/3 = (y - 7)/2 = (z - 7)/-2 = λ.

Step 2: General coordinates of foot M are: (3λ + 6, 2λ + 7, -2λ + 7).

Step 3: DRs of line PM (Subtract P(1,2,3) from M):
a1 = 3λ + 5
b1 = 2λ + 5
c1 = -2λ + 4

Step 4: DRs of the given line are a2 = 3, b2 = 2, c2 = -2.

Step 5: Apply a1a2 + b1b2 + c1c2 = 0.
3(3λ + 5) + 2(2λ + 5) - 2(-2λ + 4) = 0
9λ + 15 + 4λ + 10 + 4λ - 8 = 0
17λ + 17 = 0 ⇒ λ = -1.

Step 6: Put λ = -1 back in coordinates of M.

Application of Derivatives 3 Marks
Q16: Find the intervals in which the function f(x) = 2x3 - 3x2 - 36x + 7 is strictly increasing.

Why it is important: Increasing aur decreasing intervals ka sawal graph analysis check karne ke liye diya jata hai.

Concept: f'(x) nikalo aur use 0 ke equal rakho. Critical points ko number line par plot karo (Wavy Curve Method). Jahan f'(x) > 0 hai wahan increasing, aur jahan f'(x) < 0 wahan decreasing.



Step 1: Find the derivative f'(x).
f'(x) = 6x2 - 6x - 36

Step 2: Factorize f'(x) and equate to 0.
6(x2 - x - 6) = 0
6(x - 3)(x + 2) = 0
So, critical points are x = 3 and x = -2.

Step 3: Draw a number line and mark -2 and 3. The intervals are (-∞, -2), (-2, 3), and (3, ∞).

Step 4: Check sign of f'(x) in each interval.
For (-∞, -2): take x = -3 ⇒ f'(x) = (+) × (-) × (-) = Positive.
For (-2, 3): take x = 0 ⇒ f'(x) = (+) × (-) × (+) = Negative.
For (3, ∞): take x = 4 ⇒ f'(x) = (+) × (+) × (+) = Positive.

Final Answer: The function is strictly increasing in (-∞, -2) ∪ (3, ∞).

Probability 3 Marks
Q18: A and B are two independent events such that P(A) = 0.3 and P(A ∪ B) = 0.5. Find P(B).

Why it is important: Independent events ki condition ko test karne ke liye ye equation-based probability ka sabse common sawal hai.

Concept: Agar do events independent hote hain, toh P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B) hota hai. Ise Addition Theorem (Union formula) mein daalna hota hai.

Step 1: Use the addition formula of probability:
P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Step 2: Since A and B are independent, replace P(A ∩ B) with P(A) × P(B).
P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - [ P(A) × P(B) ]

Step 3: Put the given values. Let P(B) be 'x'.
0.5 = 0.3 + x - (0.3 × x)

Step 4: Solve for x:
0.5 - 0.3 = x - 0.3x
0.2 = 0.7x
x = 0.2 / 0.7 = 2/7

Final Answer: P(B) = 2/7

Differential Equations 5 Marks
Q19: Solve the differential equation:

(x2 - y2) dx + 2xy dy = 0

Why it is important: Homogeneous differential equation ka ek 5-mark question paper mein aana 100% tay hota hai.

Concept: Pehle dy/dx ki value nikalo. Agar har term ki degree same hai (yahan degree 2 hai), toh y = vx put karo. Phir dy/dx = v + x(dv/dx) rakh kar variables separate karo.

Step 1: Rearrange to find dy/dx:
2xy dy = -(x2 - y2) dx
dy/dx = (y2 - x2) / 2xy

Step 2: Substitute y = vx. Therefore, dy/dx = v + x(dv/dx).

Step 3: Put these in the equation:
v + x(dv/dx) = (v2x2 - x2) / (2x(vx))
v + x(dv/dx) = (v2 - 1) / 2v

Step 4: Separate variables:
x(dv/dx) = [ (v2 - 1) / 2v ] - v = (v2 - 1 - 2v2) / 2v = -(v2 + 1) / 2v

Step 5: Integrate both sides:
∫ [ 2v / (v2 + 1) ] dv = ∫ (-1/x) dx
log|v2 + 1| = -log|x| + log|C|

Step 6: Simplify using log properties:
log|(v2 + 1) × x| = log|C|
( (y2/x2) + 1 ) × x = C
(y2 + x2) / x = C

Final Answer: x2 + y2 = Cx

Step 1: Let x / ((x - 1)(x - 2)) = A / (x - 1) + B / (x - 2).

Step 2: Multiply by (x-1)(x-2):
x = A(x - 2) + B(x - 1)

Step 3: Find A and B.
Put x = 1 ⇒ 1 = A(1 - 2) ⇒ A = -1
Put x = 2 ⇒ 2 = B(2 - 1) ⇒ B = 2

Step 4: Rewrite integral:
∫ [ -1 / (x - 1) + 2 / (x - 2) ] dx

Step 5: Integrate using ∫(1/x)dx = log|x|:
= -log|x - 1| + 2log|x - 2| + C

Final Answer: log |(x - 2)2 / (x - 1)| + C

Integrals 3 Marks
Q24: Evaluate the integral using Integration by Parts:

∫ x sin x dx

Why it is important: Integration by Parts ka basic question 3 marks mein aana bahut common hai. ILATE rule lagana har student ko aana chahiye.

Concept: ILATE (Inverse, Logarithmic, Algebraic, Trigonometric, Exponential) rule se decide karo ki 1st function (u) aur 2nd function (v) kaunsa hoga. Formula: ∫ u v dx = u ∫ v dx - ∫ [ (du/dx) ∫ v dx ] dx.

Step 1: Identify 'u' and 'v' using ILATE.
'x' is Algebraic (A) and 'sin x' is Trigonometric (T). So, u = x and v = sin x.

Step 2: Apply the formula:
= x ∫ sin x dx - ∫ [ (d/dx(x)) ∫ sin x dx ] dx

Step 3: Integrate:
= x (-cos x) - ∫ [ 1 × (-cos x) ] dx
= -x cos x + ∫ cos x dx

Step 4: Final integration:
= -x cos x + sin x + C

Final Answer: sin x - x cos x + C

Q26: Let A = R - {3} and B = R - {1}. Consider the function f: A → B defined by f(x) = (x - 2) / (x - 3). Show that f is one-one and onto.

x1x2 - 3x1 - 2x2 + 6 = x1x2 - 3x2 - 2x1 + 6

Q46: If A is a skew-symmetric matrix of odd order (say order 3), write the value of its determinant |A|.

Why it is important: Yeh 1 mark ka MCQ trick question hai jisme koi calculation nahi karni hoti, bas property yaad honi chahiye.

Shortcut Trick: Kisi bhi Skew-Symmetric matrix ka diagonal hamesha 0 hota hai, aur agar uska order odd (jaise 3x3 ya 5x5) hai, toh uska determinant humesha ZERO (0) hota hai.

Step 1: By definition of skew-symmetric matrix, AT = -A.

Step 2: Take determinant on both sides:
|AT| = |-A|

Step 3: We know |AT| = |A|, and |-A| = (-1)n|A| where n is order.

Step 4: Since n is odd (e.g., 3), (-1)3 = -1.
So, |A| = -|A|

Step 5: 2|A| = 0 ⇒ |A| = 0.

Final Answer: 0

Definite Integrals 4 Marks
Q47: Evaluate: ∫0π/4 log(1 + tan x) dx

Why it is important: CBSE ka "King Property" par base sabse famous aur repeated question.

Concept: Property ∫0a f(x) dx = ∫0a f(a - x) dx lagao. tan(π/4 - x) ka formula kholo aur dono equations ko add kar do, log ki terms cancel ho jayengi.

Step 1: Let I = ∫0π/4 log(1 + tan x) dx --- (Eq 1)

Step 2: Apply property (replace x with π/4 - x):
I = ∫0π/4 log[ 1 + tan(π/4 - x) ] dx

Step 3: Use formula tan(A-B) = (tanA - tanB)/(1 + tanA tanB):
I = ∫0π/4 log[ 1 + (1 - tan x)/(1 + tan x) ] dx

Step 4: Take LCM inside log:
I = ∫0π/4 log[ (1 + tan x + 1 - tan x) / (1 + tan x) ] dx
I = ∫0π/4 log[ 2 / (1 + tan x) ] dx

Step 5: Use log(m/n) = log m - log n:
I = ∫0π/4 [ log 2 - log(1 + tan x) ] dx --- (Eq 2)

Step 6: Add Eq 1 and Eq 2:
2I = ∫0π/4 log 2 dx
2I = log 2 [x]0π/4 = (π/4) log 2
I = (π/8) log 2

Final Answer: (π/8) log 2

Vector Algebra 3 Marks
Q48: Find the value of λ if the vectors a = i + 3j + k, b = 2i - j - k, and c = λi + 7j + 3k are coplanar.

Why it is important: Coplanar vectors ka Scalar Triple Product (STP) par base ek solid 3-mark question.

Concept: Agar 3 vectors same plane mein hain (coplanar), toh unse banne wale box (parallelepiped) ka volume ZERO hota hai. Iska matlab unka determinant 0 ke equal rakhna hai.

Step 1: Condition for coplanarity: [a b c] = 0. This means determinant is 0.

Step 2: Set up the determinant.
| 1 3 1 |
| 2 -1 -1 | = 0
| λ 7 3 |

Step 3: Expand the determinant along the first row:
1(-3 - (-7)) - 3(6 - (-\lambda)) + 1(14 - (-\lambda)) = 0

Step 4: Simplify:
1(-3 + 7) - 3(6 + λ) + 1(14 + λ) = 0
4 - 18 - 3λ + 14 + λ = 0
(4 - 18 + 14) - 2λ = 0

Step 5: Solve for λ:
0 - 2λ = 0 ⇒ λ = 0.

Final Answer: λ = 0.

Differential Equations 4 Marks
Q49: Find the particular solution of the differential equation:

dy/dx = y tan x, given that y = 1 when x = 0.

Why it is important: "Particular solution" nikalna bahut scoring hota hai kyunki "C" ki value nikalne ke extra marks milte hain. Ye Variable Separable ka aasan question hai.

Concept: 'y' wali terms ko 'dy' ke paas le jao aur 'x' wali terms ko 'dx' ke paas. Integrate karke Constant 'C' aayega. Phir x aur y ki given values daal kar 'C' nikalo aur final equation me wapas rakh do.

Step 1: Separate variables:
dy/y = tan x dx

Step 2: Integrate both sides:
∫ (1/y) dy = ∫ tan x dx

Step 3: Use standard integration formulas:
log|y| = log|sec x| + C

Step 4: Find 'C'. Put y = 1 and x = 0.
log(1) = log(sec 0) + C
0 = log(1) + C ⇒ 0 = 0 + C ⇒ C = 0.

Step 5: Put C back into the equation:
log|y| = log|sec x| + 0
log|y| = log|sec x|

Step 6: Remove log from both sides:
y = sec x.

Final Answer: y = sec x.

Relations & Functions 2/3 Marks
Q50: Let R be the equivalence relation in the set A = {0, 1, 2, ..., 12} given by R = {(a, b) : |a - b| is a multiple of 4}. Find the equivalence class of [1].

Why it is important: Relation and Functions mein Equivalence Class nikalna naya topic hai aur board exams mein regularly pucha ja raha hai.

Concept: [1] ka matlab hai wo saare numbers (a) jo 1 ke sath relation banate hain. Matlab |a - 1| ek multiple of 4 hona chahiye (0, 4, 8, 12). Aur 'a' ki value set A ke andar (0 se 12) hi honi chahiye.

Step 1: We need to find all elements 'x' in set A such that (x, 1) belongs to R.

Step 2: This means |x - 1| should be a multiple of 4.

Step 3: The multiples of 4 are 0, 4, 8, 12, ...
So, |x - 1| = 0 ⇒ x = 1.
|x - 1| = 4 ⇒ x - 1 = ±4 ⇒ x = 5 (since -3 is not in A).
|x - 1| = 8 ⇒ x - 1 = ±8 ⇒ x = 9 (since -7 is not in A).
|x - 1| = 12 ⇒ x - 1 = ±12 ⇒ x = 13 (Rejected, as 13 is not in A).

Step 4: Collect all valid values of x from Set A.

Final Answer: Equivalence class [1] = {1, 5, 9}.
